Common SCPs

SCP-173 – "The Sculpture"

Containment: Kept in a locked container. Two personnel must maintain eye contact at all times.

Strength: Moves incredibly fast when not observed. Can snap necks instantly.

Weakness: Cannot move when observed.

SCP-096 – "The Shy Guy"

Containment: Must be contained in a sealed, soundproof cell. Cameras must not view its face.

Strength: Near-invincible when enraged. Will pursue targets across the globe.

Weakness: Only triggered when someone sees its face.

SCP-457 – "Burning Man"

Containment: Housed in fire-retardant chamber with CO₂ suppression system.

Strength: Consumes heat and grows stronger. Immune to fire.

Weakness: Can be weakened with lack of fuel or oxygen.

SCP-999 – "The Tickle Monster"

Containment: Allowed to roam under observation. Harmless.

Strength: Emits euphoria, cures depression. Potential counter-agent to 682.

Weakness: None currently known.

Containment Protocols

  • All SCPs are to be secured in soundproof, reinforced cells.
  • Electronic surveillance must be installed in every containment chamber.
  • Entry requires dual biometric clearance from Level 4+ staff.
  • Mobile Task Forces (MTF) must be on standby at all times.
  • Weekly security audits are mandatory. Breaches trigger lockdowns within 3 seconds.
  • Failure to comply results in disciplinary action or demotion.

Site-64 Lore

Located in Portland, Oregon, Site-64 is a mid-size secure containment and research site tasked with Pacific Northwest anomalies. Known for its proximity to active anomalous zones and frequent deployment of MTF Psi-7 ("Home Improvement").

Founded post-WWII, Site-64 originally operated under Project Bluebook. Its long history includes the containment of early Keter-class threats and housing O5 outposts.
